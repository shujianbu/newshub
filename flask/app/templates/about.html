About Newshub

Newshub is a tool to watch different versions of online news articles in Asia.

In today’s information-driven world, news websites publish several thousand stories a day. Many times, particularly in authoritarian regimes, changes are made to articles without any communication to readers. This can be due to political interference, or a nexus between the media and influential entities.

We embarked on this project to expose censorship and inappropriate journalistic practice post-publication and engage the public to hold journalism accountable.

Asia-focused

There are several things we still don't have clear answers to when it comes to the media landscape in Asia.

We know that censorship exists in China, but don't know how much of it is post-publication. We know that the government of Singapore controls the media, but we don't know how often is an article deleted completely, or altered substantially to reflect the government's viewpoint.

In India, the media is largely free, but is increasingly being influenced by big businesses. How many times does pressure from big business result in editors changing the tone of a story, or taking out names of influential people from controversial stories?

Our goal

Through Newshub we plan to have answers to these questions that have been in the realm of speculation for years. For now, we are going to track changes in news websites in English and Mandarin Chinese, but over time plan to expand it to multiple languages to broaden our scope to other regions where the media is being unduly influenced.

How we do it

Instead of creating individual scripts for each websites we track, which can be time consuming and cumbersome, we have used a new python module to help pull out content of news articles from over 50 news websites in Asia.

We will produce monthly news reports and visualizations to help our readers better understand the data we collect and analyze. Ultimately, Newshub is for you, the reader, to help hold your newssources accountable. Welcome aboard.

Newshub is the joint effort of recent graduates from Columbia Graduate School of Journalism and Columbia the Fu Foundation School of Engineeringa and Applied Science.

The project has been funded through Magic Grant, awarded annually by the Brown Institue for Media Innovation, a collaboration between Columbia Univ ersity and Stanford University.

We welcome any suggestions or questions on the publication selection, data interpretation, algorithm, etc. If you have any stories about censorship and inappropriate journalistic practice post-publication, please also contact us at info@newshub.cc
